# Personas and Buying Committee

## Persona map

Handout is commonly a bottom-up seller tool with a manager, operations, or enablement buyer. The person who feels the pain most intensely may not own budget or security approval.

Typical roles:

- Daily user: account executive, founder, business-development rep, partner manager, consultant.
- Champion: high-performing seller, sales enablement lead, RevOps operator, founder.
- Economic buyer: VP Sales, CRO, founder, agency owner, services leader.
- Operational buyer: RevOps or Sales Enablement.
- Technical/security reviewer: IT, security, privacy, legal.
- Influencer: marketing, brand, customer success, solutions consulting.

## Persona 1: Account Executive

### Goals

- Keep momentum after discovery or demo.
- Make the follow-up feel specific to the account.
- Give the champion something easy to forward.
- Know when and how to follow up.
- Spend more time selling and less time assembling collateral.

### Current frustrations

- Hunting for the right links and proof points.
- Editing decks or docs for every account.
- Long follow-up emails that buyers do not read.
- No visibility after sending.
- Conflicting versions of pricing or product material.
- Buyers asking for information already sent.

### What resonates

- “Build once, personalize in seconds.”
- “One place for the buyer’s story, proof, and next step.”
- “Insert a recipient-specific card directly from Gmail.”
- “Know which explicit actions happened after the link opened.”

### Likely objections

- “This is another tool I have to maintain.”
- “I can just send a deck.”
- “I do not have time to build pages.”
- “Will buyers actually click it?”

### Proof to show

- Start from an existing reusable site.
- Create a recipient with name/company/pain point.
- Insert the card into Gmail in one flow.
- Open the link and show the resulting session/actions.

## Persona 2: Sales Development or Business Development Rep

### Goals

- Make outreach and follow-up stand out.
- Reuse approved messaging.
- Book the next meeting.
- Personalize without adding heavy research time.

### Best use cases

- Post-conversation follow-up.
- Event/intent follow-up.
- Named-account outbound after a response.
- Curated “why this matters” page for a high-priority account.

### Caution

Handout is not an automated sequencing tool, contact database, or email-pixel system. It is better for high-value, contextual follow-up than indiscriminate mass outreach.

## Persona 3: Founder or founder-led seller

### Goals

- Look polished without hiring a full enablement/design function.
- Tailor the story to strategic prospects.
- Learn what resonates while the motion is still evolving.
- Turn founder knowledge into a reusable sales asset.

### What resonates

- “Turn the way you sell into a reusable buyer experience.”
- “Keep the message custom without rebuilding it from scratch.”
- “Draft up to 10 sites free and upgrade when you are ready to publish.”
- “Use an AI agent to help create and personalize the site through MCP.”

### Buying posture

Founder-led buyers can move quickly if the product produces a customer-ready outcome in the first session. Avoid overloading them with governance language; demonstrate immediate leverage.

## Persona 4: VP Sales or Head of Sales

### Goals

- Improve conversion and sales-cycle velocity.
- Make strong follow-up repeatable across the team.
- Reduce dependence on a few excellent reps.
- See real buyer engagement without encouraging invasive tactics.
- Increase multi-threading and champion effectiveness.

### Current frustrations

- Managers cannot see the quality of what reps send.
- Follow-up is inconsistent.
- Enablement content is underused.
- Deals stall after a good demo.
- CRM stages do not reveal buyer behavior.

### What resonates

- Standardized master site with recipient-level flexibility.
- One buyer destination that the champion can forward.
- Sessions and modeled action signals for coaching and timing.
- Lightweight deployment compared with a heavyweight deal-room project.

### Questions they will ask

- Will reps adopt it?
- How quickly can a site be created?
- Can enablement control the base content?
- What signals do managers get?
- Does it integrate with our workflow?
- What is the per-seat cost and expected value?

### Proof to show

- Demonstrate the fastest rep workflow, not only editor depth.
- Show one master site serving multiple recipients.
- Connect engagement to a realistic follow-up decision.
- Build a pilot around one motion and measurable adoption/velocity outcomes.

## Persona 5: Sales Enablement

### Goals

- Turn approved messaging and proof into usable seller workflows.
- Increase content adoption.
- Keep materials current.
- Give reps flexibility without losing governance.
- Accelerate onboarding and new-play launches.

### What resonates

- Reusable sites instead of rep-owned copies.
- Variables for controlled personalization.
- Content blocks for proof, testimonials, product, implementation, and next steps.
- Multi-page structures for standard playbooks.
- Agent-assisted generation based on approved inputs.

### Likely objections

- “Can I lock content or define templates?”
- “Can I see who changed what?”
- “Can I distribute approved sites to the team?”
- “Does it support roles and permissions?”

### Honest response

The product architecture supports workspace ownership, collaborative editing, roles, and reusable master sites. Template and granular governance capabilities should be confirmed in the live environment before being presented as complete. Focus the initial pilot on one centrally maintained site and a small seller group.

## Persona 6: Revenue Operations

### Goals

- Reduce manual steps and tool sprawl.
- Improve process consistency and observability.
- Control costs and permissions.
- Connect buyer signals to a usable seller action.
- Enable automation without creating brittle shadow systems.

### What resonates

- One canonical site plus recipient overrides.
- Structured recipient and tracking data.
- Retention and internal-traffic controls.
- MCP tools for programmatic workflows.
- Workspace-owned billing and publishing rules.

### Likely objections

- “Where is the CRM integration?”
- “How are recipients matched?”
- “Can we automate site creation?”
- “What data is stored?”
- “Can we export or webhook activity?”

### Honest response

- CRM enrichment and sync are not current confirmed capabilities.
- The recipient model intentionally does not store recipient email in V1.
- MCP supports programmatic creation and personalization.
- Current tracking stores sessions and explicit events without raw IP, persistent device IDs, page paths, or typed values.
- Webhook event types exist in the tracking model, but customer-facing webhook product availability should be confirmed.

## Persona 7: Agency owner, consultant, or services leader

### Goals

- Increase perceived quality.
- Turn discovery into a clear recommendation.
- Give buyers a concise, forwardable proposal companion.
- Standardize methodology while preserving a bespoke feel.
- Shorten the time from call to polished follow-up.

### What resonates

- Personalized, branded experience without custom design work.
- Pages for approach, scope, proof, timeline, and next steps.
- Video, testimonial, logo, table, task-list, and booking content.
- Recipient link with engagement signals.

### Caution

Do not position Handout as a replacement for contracts, e-signature, invoices, or project delivery portals.

## Persona 8: Partner or channel manager

### Goals

- Make joint offerings easy to understand.
- Equip partners or referrals with a clear narrative.
- Coordinate next steps across multiple organizations.
- See whether shared material generated engagement.

### What resonates

- Duo/partnership visual treatment.
- Reusable joint-solution site.
- Account variables and stable links.
- Forwardable experience with explicit next steps.

## Persona 9: Marketing or brand stakeholder

### Goals

- Protect brand consistency.
- Ensure approved proof and messaging are used.
- Avoid uncontrolled rep-created microsites.
- Keep the public buyer experience polished and responsive.

### Possible tension

Marketing may fear that a seller-facing builder creates off-brand content. Address this by showing the canonical design system, constrained content model, shared renderer, theme modes, and the value of a centrally maintained master site. Do not overstate template locking or formal approval workflows.

## Persona 10: Security, privacy, legal, and IT

### Goals

- Understand public-link exposure.
- Minimize data collection.
- Review email-extension permissions.
- Confirm retention and deletion behavior.
- Validate subprocessors, contracts, and compliance posture.

### What resonates

- First-party event tracking.
- No session replay.
- No cookies or persistent device IDs in the tracking design.
- No raw IP storage.
- No email body, subject, thread, or attachment access in the Gmail extension.
- Site-level tracking controls, retention choices, and internal-network suppression.
- Immutable published versions and signed tracking context.

### Boundary

Do not claim certifications, DPAs, legal compliance, data residency, uptime commitments, or completed production controls unless current external documentation confirms them.

## Buying-committee alignment

| Stakeholder | Desired outcome | Best proof | Risk to address |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Seller | Faster, better follow-up | Gmail-to-recipient demo | Extra work/tool |
| Manager | Consistency and conversion | Reusable master + activity | Adoption |
| Enablement | Governed reusable content | Variables and multi-page structure | Control maturity |
| RevOps | Structured workflow and data | Recipient model, MCP, tracking | CRM gap |
| Marketing | Brand-safe output | Shared rendering and themes | Rep-created content |
| Security/privacy | Minimal collection | Tracking and extension boundaries | Public links/compliance |
| Finance | Clear seat economics | Pilot business case | Per-seat spend |

## Champion profile

The best champion:

- Personally experiences the follow-up problem.
- Owns or strongly influences one repeatable sales motion.
- Can recruit 3–10 sellers for a pilot.
- Has access to approved content and proof.
- Can define a measurable baseline.
- Values buyer experience and privacy.
- Can navigate security and procurement if the pilot expands.

## Multi-threading guidance

Do not rely on seller enthusiasm alone. By the second serious conversation, seek:

- A manager or economic buyer for the business case.
- RevOps/enablement for workflow and rollout.
- Security/privacy if the company has formal review.
- Marketing/brand if public presentation is tightly controlled.

Position each conversation around that stakeholder’s job rather than repeating the same product tour.
