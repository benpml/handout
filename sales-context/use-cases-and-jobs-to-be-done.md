# Use Cases and Jobs to Be Done

## Core job to be done

> When a prospect needs more information after a sales interaction, help me turn the relevant story, proof, and next step into one polished, personalized destination, send it quickly, and know enough about engagement to follow up intelligently.

## Use-case selection rule

The best Handout use case has:

- A repeatable base narrative.
- A meaningful account-specific layer.
- More than one asset or section.
- A specific next action.
- A reason to know whether the buyer engaged.

## 1. Post-discovery follow-up

### Trigger

The first substantive call ends with “send me more information” or an agreement to explore a problem.

### Current workflow

Seller writes a recap email, attaches a deck, and adds several links. The buyer receives information but not a clear decision path.

### Handout workflow

- Use a reusable discovery-follow-up site.
- Personalize the recipient/company and top pain point.
- Add a short “what we heard” section.
- Include the most relevant product/approach explanation.
- Add proof tied to the pain.
- End with a booking link or agreed next step.
- Insert the recipient card from Gmail.

### Recommended pages

- Overview.
- What we heard.
- How Handout/customer solution helps.
- Relevant proof.
- Next steps.

### Value

- Shows the seller listened.
- Makes the follow-up easy to forward.
- Creates a visible next step.
- Gives the seller a signal before chasing the buyer.

## 2. Post-demo recap

### Trigger

A demo covered several workflows, but only some were relevant to the account.

### Handout workflow

- Summarize the buyer’s priority workflows.
- Embed a short relevant video or GIF.
- Highlight the specific product moments that mattered.
- Add a customer quote or logo grid.
- Include implementation or evaluation steps.
- Use recipient variables for company, pain, and CTA.

### Engagement interpretation

- A site visit confirms the recap was opened.
- Tab switches reveal which logical sections received interaction.
- Modeled link/button clicks show explicit actions.
- Absence of activity is not proof of disinterest; delivery and access should be checked.

## 3. Executive summary and champion enablement

### Trigger

The champion needs to bring an executive or cross-functional stakeholder into the decision.

### Handout workflow

- Create an executive-first summary page.
- State the business problem and desired outcome in buyer language.
- Keep product detail subordinate to value, risk, and next decision.
- Include a concise business case and proof.
- Give the champion one forwardable link.

### Value

- Reduces the champion’s burden of retelling the story.
- Maintains message consistency when forwarded.
- Keeps detailed supporting material one tab away.

## 4. Pricing and packaging follow-up

### Trigger

The buyer needs to understand options, scope, or investment after a commercial conversation.

### Handout workflow

- Present the recommended option first.
- Use a table for plan/scope comparison.
- Personalize the recommended plan, assumptions, or CTA.
- Include value rationale and what is included.
- Link to a call or next-step review.

### Boundary

Handout is a presentation and follow-up layer, not CPQ, invoicing, contracting, or e-signature. Prices and terms should come from an authoritative commercial source.

## 5. Business case or ROI narrative

### Trigger

The buyer needs internal justification.

### Handout workflow

- Frame the current process and cost.
- State assumptions transparently.
- Present expected operational or revenue impact.
- Include qualitative proof and implementation requirements.
- Make the next validation step explicit.

### Recommended variables

- Company.
- Team size.
- Current time/cost baseline.
- Priority outcome.
- Recommended scope.
- Executive CTA.

### Boundary

Never invent ROI. Use customer-provided assumptions and label estimates clearly.

## 6. Mutual action plan or evaluation plan

### Trigger

The deal has multiple steps, stakeholders, and due dates.

### Handout workflow

- Use a task list for agreed actions.
- Add pages for requirements, evaluation criteria, stakeholders, and next steps.
- Link to relevant security, implementation, and product resources.
- Keep the public site focused on the current evaluation.

### Boundary

Handout task lists are content in a buyer site, not established as a bidirectional project-management system with buyer editing, reminders, approvals, or dependencies.

## 7. Security and procurement handoff

### Trigger

The buyer introduces security, legal, IT, or procurement.

### Handout workflow

- Create a clear security/implementation page.
- Link only to approved resources.
- Summarize architecture or data handling in non-technical language.
- Include the owner and next step.
- Use a recipient-specific link for attribution.

### Boundary

Do not place secrets, credentials, private reports, regulated data, or confidential legal material on a public Handout unless the public-link model and customer policy explicitly permit it. Handout is not established here as an authenticated data room.

## 8. Implementation overview

### Trigger

The buyer understands the product but is unsure about effort, rollout, or risk.

### Handout workflow

- Present phases and owners.
- Use icon cards or a table for milestones.
- Embed relevant technical or training material.
- Show expected time-to-value.
- Link to the next implementation review.

### Value

- Addresses fear of change.
- Gives technical and operational stakeholders a clear path.
- Makes implementation part of the value story rather than an appendix.

## 9. Case-study share

### Trigger

The buyer wants evidence from similar customers or situations.

### Handout workflow

- Curate only the most relevant proof.
- Use testimonials, logo grids, images, and video.
- Explain why each example is relevant to this buyer.
- Personalize the framing, not the underlying verified facts.

### Boundary

Use only approved, accurate customer names, logos, quotes, and outcomes. Do not fabricate placeholder proof in an external site.

## 10. Partner and co-selling follow-up

### Trigger

Two companies are presenting a joint solution or a partner refers an account.

### Handout workflow

- Use a partnership title/logo treatment.
- Explain each party’s role.
- Personalize the joint value for the recipient.
- Add shared proof and a single next step.
- Keep ownership clear.

## 11. Agency or consulting proposal companion

### Trigger

A service provider needs a polished explanation of approach, team, proof, timeline, and next steps.

### Handout workflow

- Summarize the client’s situation.
- Present the recommended approach.
- Show relevant work and testimonials.
- Include scope/timeline context.
- Link to the formal proposal or meeting.

### Boundary

The Handout complements, but does not replace, a formal statement of work, contract, e-signature, or payment flow.

## 12. New product or segment play

### Trigger

Sales leadership launches a new product, vertical, or persona motion.

### Handout workflow

- Enablement creates a reusable master site.
- Variables encode the appropriate recipient/account layer.
- Reps personalize and share through a consistent flow.
- Engagement signals inform iteration on the narrative and CTA.

### Value

- Faster rollout than building custom marketing pages.
- More controlled than rep-built decks.
- Easier to learn from than static collateral.

## 13. Event or conference follow-up

### Trigger

A seller has a real conversation at an event and needs a relevant follow-up.

### Handout workflow

- Use a short event-follow-up site.
- Personalize the person, company, topic, and promised resource.
- Insert from Gmail shortly after the conversation.
- Track the recipient link and follow up based on activity.

### Boundary

Avoid turning Handout into a mass-event email blast. Its strength is contextual, named-account follow-up.

## 14. Account-based outbound after engagement

### Trigger

The account has shown a high-value signal and deserves a tailored outbound experience.

### Handout workflow

- Create a concise account hypothesis.
- Personalize the business problem and relevant proof.
- Include one clear CTA.
- Send a descriptive link, not a generic “click here.”

### Boundary

Handout does not supply intent data, contacts, enrichment, or sequencing in the current product evidence. It is the destination and engagement layer once the seller has a reason to engage.

## 15. Customer expansion and renewal

### Trigger

An existing customer is evaluating expansion, renewal, or a new use case.

### Handout workflow

- Summarize outcomes and current adoption using approved data.
- Present the expansion opportunity.
- Tailor proof and implementation to the customer.
- Use a recipient link for the decision group.

### Boundary

Handout is not a customer-success platform or source of product-usage analytics. Import customer facts only from authoritative systems.

## 16. AI-assisted personalized site creation

### Trigger

A team wants an AI sales agent to prepare buyer-facing follow-up based on approved inputs.

### Handout workflow

- Agent reads current capabilities and site content.
- Agent selects or creates a site.
- Agent writes canonical content using approved audience, offer, CTA, and proof.
- Agent validates the site.
- Agent creates recipient variants in batch.
- Publishing happens only with explicit authorization.
- Agent returns public URLs and, later, tracking summaries.

### Value

- Reduces repetitive production work.
- Keeps personalization structured.
- Preserves validation and publish boundaries.

### Boundary

The agent must not invent proof, publish without authorization, override permissions, or treat tracking as proof of identity beyond the recipient-link context.

## Recommended site anatomy by motion

| Motion | Page 1 | Page 2 | Page 3 | Primary CTA |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Discovery follow-up | What we heard | Relevant approach | Proof | Book next step |
| Demo recap | Priority workflows | Demo highlights | Evaluation plan | Schedule review |
| Executive summary | Business outcome | Why now | Proof/risk | Executive alignment |
| Pricing | Recommendation | Options | Value/terms | Commercial review |
| Implementation | Plan | Responsibilities | Timeline/resources | Implementation call |
| Agency proposal | Situation | Approach/scope | Team/proof | Review proposal |
| Partner motion | Joint value | Roles | Proof | Joint call |

## Success measures by use case

Measure the workflow, not only page traffic:

- Time from call end to polished follow-up.
- Percentage of target opportunities receiving a Handout.
- Recipient-site open/session rate where measurable.
- Modeled CTA click rate.
- Time from share to next meeting.
- Number of stakeholders engaged or introduced, using explicit seller data rather than inferred identity.
- Seller reuse rate of approved master sites.
- Reduction in duplicated decks/docs/pages.
- Qualitative buyer feedback on clarity and forwardability.
- Stage conversion and cycle time for a controlled pilot cohort.

## Use-case anti-patterns

Avoid using Handout as:

- An SEO website or campaign CMS.
- A mass email-tracking pixel.
- An anonymous visitor-deanonymization tool.
- A contract or e-signature platform.
- A CRM or sequencing platform.
- A private data room for sensitive documents.
- A session-replay tool.
- A replacement for every deck or document.

Use it where a seller-shared, personalized buyer experience materially improves the next decision.
