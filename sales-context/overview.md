# Handout Sales Context

This folder is the sales-facing source of context for Handout. It is written for founders, sellers, partners, and AI sales agents that need to understand the product well enough to position it accurately, qualify prospects, run discovery, demonstrate the product, handle objections, and avoid unsupported claims.

## The shortest accurate description

Handout helps B2B sales teams turn scattered follow-up material into a polished, personalized buyer site, send a recipient-specific link in seconds, and see the meaningful actions that happen after the link is opened.

The product combines:

- A collaborative, Notion-like editor for compact sales sites.
- Reusable personalization through variables rather than duplicated pages.
- Stable recipient links with recipient-specific presentation and attribution.
- Gmail-native creation and sharing through a Chrome extension build.
- Privacy-conscious, first-party engagement tracking for sessions and modeled actions.
- An MCP/agent surface for programmatic creation, personalization, publishing, and tracking reads.

## Claim-status model

Every seller should distinguish these states:

- **Verified product capability:** Supported by current code and/or an implementation-status document in this repository.
- **Implemented build, distribution unconfirmed:** The product exists in the codebase, but public availability, production rollout, or customer access has not been independently confirmed here.
- **Product direction or specified behavior:** Described in a product specification but not safe to present as generally available without confirmation.
- **Sales recommendation:** Positioning, ICP, qualification, or messaging derived from the product. It is guidance, not a product fact.
- **Do not promise:** Explicitly excluded, deferred, removed, or unsupported by the current product evidence.

When in doubt, say: “The product is designed to support that workflow; I’ll confirm the exact availability for your workspace.”

## Core sales principles

- Sell the change in the buyer experience, not “a prettier link.”
- Lead with one reusable buyer-facing destination, fast personalization, and visible engagement.
- Show the workflow end to end: build once, personalize, share, observe, follow up.
- Do not imply that Handout knows a visitor’s identity unless the seller used a recipient-specific link.
- Do not call current event tracking “session replay.” Replay data is explicitly excluded from the current tracking design.
- Do not present preview-bot activity as proof that a human shared or viewed a link.
- Do not promise CRM enrichment, automatic follow-up, form capture, custom domains, booking-completion tracking, or enterprise certifications unless separately confirmed.
- Avoid made-up customer logos, outcomes, ROI, integrations, or testimonials. Use a prospect’s own workflow and baseline to construct a business case.

## Updating this pack

Product behavior changes quickly. Before external use, refresh these areas against the live product:

- Pricing and packaging.
- Production availability of the Gmail extension.
- Production availability and authentication of the MCP/agent surface.
- Team administration and permissions.
- Tracking event types and retention defaults.
- Any security certifications, legal terms, or data-processing commitments.
- Any new integrations, templates, or public-domain features.
